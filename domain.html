Research Domain

Federated Learning for Distributed Skin Disease Prediction

A synthesis of four research tracks covering bacterial, fungal, viral, and parasitic dermatological classification under a shared privacy-preserving learning framework.

Literature Survey

Recent dermatology research shows strong classification performance from convolutional neural networks and transfer learning, particularly with compact architectures such as MobileNetV2. In parallel, federated learning has become increasingly relevant in healthcare because it allows institutions to collaborate without directly sharing sensitive medical data.

Existing studies often target a single disease category, a single dataset, or centralized training assumptions. The combined use of federated learning and clinically practical CNN deployment for dermatological imaging remains comparatively underexplored.

Research Gap

There is no unified, lightweight federated framework in the reviewed work that jointly addresses bacterial, fungal, viral, and parasitic skin disease prediction while preserving patient privacy and maintaining deployable inference efficiency.

Research Problem

Medical institutions need accurate skin disease prediction models, but centralizing image datasets creates privacy, compliance, and governance risks. The challenge is to achieve strong image classification performance across distributed clients without exposing raw dermatological data.

Research Objectives

Objective 1

Develop a bacterial skin disease classifier using transfer learning on MobileNetV2.

Objective 2

Design a fungal skin disease prediction pipeline adapted for federated local training.

Objective 3

Train viral and parasitic image classifiers with decentralized client participation and shared aggregation.

Objective 4

Integrate all disease modules within a federated learning workflow using FedAvg, secure APIs, and scalable deployment patterns.

Methodology

1. Local Data Preparation

Each institution maintains its own skin image dataset and preprocessing pipeline.

2. Local Model Training

Clients fine-tune MobileNetV2 models on their local data using transfer learning.

3. Federated Aggregation

Model parameters are transmitted to a coordinating server and combined using FedAvg.

4. Global Model Distribution

The aggregated model is redistributed to clients for iterative training rounds and evaluation.
